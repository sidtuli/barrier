What is Barrier?

"Barrier" is a capstone project focused on issues of internet access and censorship. Each region has its own video that varies depending on the presence or lack internet availability and press freedom in an area.

Differences Among Regions

The differences are all to show how the message of a story or access to information faces several barriers from censorship and a lack of media access. The more censorship present the region, the more information goes missing or unseen to the public. When internet access is not widely available, it is harder to acquire information. People either lose out on information due to this and never get to access certain news due to slow speeds or lack of a medium to get it. They may even have to pay more to have the ability to get information or will have to inconvenience themselves in other ways. If you would like to know, I have given a full explanation of differences here.

The percentages on the ethernet cord were placed just to allow viewers on choppier videos to relatively know how far they were along on the travel section.

Statistics and Research

Under each video there is a series of statistics.For countries it is their press freedom index ranking, according to Reporters Without Borders and average bandwidth, as reported by Akamai Technologies.

The statistics underneath regions in the United States tell of what percentage of people use each technology, according to the FCC.
