What is Barrier?

"Barrier" is a capstone project focused on issues of internet access and censorship. Each region has its own video that varies depending on the presence or lack internet availability and press freedom in an area.

Differences Among Regions

As you've seen in each region, there are a variety of diferences in the videos of each location, so I will explain. The choppiness of videos and the delay of revealing statistics are tied directly to the average speed of internet in the area. Having a delay on revealing statistics, the amount of video blacked out, and the portion of an ending quote missing all relate to censorship present in the area.Whether or not the narrative goes to the library or book store is a reflection of internet infrastructure. I believe internet service is a public good so then if it is widely available, by government policy or public investment, it is affordable to the community, like a library. If the reverse is true then that means that it is expensive to use the internet or it comes at additional burden to the user, like a book store. That is shown with the difference of using money or a library card to access the book at the final exchange. Additionally, the audio of a bus, public transportation, or walking, a lack ofpublic transportation, was again stressing how the internet is a public service that is not properly supported in cerain places.

The percentages on the ethernet cord were placed just to allow viewers on choppier videos to relatively know how far they were along on the travel section.

Statistics and Research

Under each video there is a series of statistics.For countries it is their press freedom index ranking, according to Reporters Without Borders and average bandwidth, as reported byAkamai Technologies.

The statistics underneath regions in the United States tell of what percentage of people use each technology, according to the FCC.
